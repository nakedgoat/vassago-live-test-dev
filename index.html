VASSAGO ANSWERGrounded in 8 cited sources

Open source AI is entering a practical infrastructure phase. The story is no longer only about releasing larger models; it is about making capable models cheaper, auditable, and useful in specific environments.1

Three shifts matter most

  • Smaller, specialized models are closing the quality gap for focused tasks while reducing compute and latency.1,2
  • Local-first deployment is improving privacy and giving teams more control over sensitive workflows.3
  • Evaluation is becoming infrastructure. Reproducible benchmarks and provenance now matter as much as raw capability.2,4

The useful distinction: “Open” can describe code, model weights, training data, or licensing. Compare those dimensions separately.
